Overview

Mark My Page is a bookmark management app designed to help you organize, tag, and track your progress across websites and online resources. We are committed to your privacy. This policy explains clearly what data we collect — and more importantly, what we do not.

🔒

Data We Do Not Collect

Mark My Page does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal information from users. Specifically, we do not collect:

  • Your name, email address, or any account credentials
  • Your browsing history or activity outside the app
  • Device identifiers, IP addresses, or location data
  • Analytics, usage statistics, or behavioral data
  • Any information entered into websites you visit
All MarkIT data — tags, bookmarks, and reading progress — are stored locally on your device and are never transmitted to our servers.
☁️

iCloud Sync (Optional)

If you are signed into iCloud on your device, MarkIT may use Apple's CloudKit to sync your bookmarks and tags across your own devices. This sync happens entirely through Apple's infrastructure — we never receive, access, or store your iCloud data. Apple's privacy policy governs this sync.

You can disable iCloud sync at any time via Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → MarkIT.

🌐

Third-Party Websites & Your Responsibility

Mark My Page acts solely as a bookmark and progress tracker. When you open a link within the app, you are navigating to an external website that is entirely outside our control.

Third-party websites — including developer documentation portals, blogs, SaaS platforms, and any other URLs you bookmark — may independently collect information about you. This may include:

  • Cookies and tracking scripts
  • Login credentials and session data
  • IP address and device information
  • Usage patterns and analytics
Mark My Page is not responsible for the privacy practices or data collection policies of any website you visit. You are solely responsible for reviewing and understanding the privacy policy of each website you access.
